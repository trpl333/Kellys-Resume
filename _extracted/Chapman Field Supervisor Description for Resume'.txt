One Version:
Chapman University – Special Education & Multiple Subject Graduate Candidate Field Supervisor
Role Overview
The Field Supervisor supports and evaluates graduate teacher candidates during their clinical fieldwork and student teaching placements. This role serves as a liaison between the university, school site, and candidate, ensuring alignment with program standards, professional expectations, and state credentialing requirements. The Field Supervisor provides coaching, formal observation, and performance-based feedback to promote candidate growth and readiness for independent teaching.
Core Responsibilities
1. Candidate Supervision & Observation
Conduct required field observations (typically a minimum of six formal observations across the placement)
Provide timely, specific, and actionable feedback aligned to Teaching Performance Expectations (TPEs)
Facilitate pre- and post-observation conferences to guide reflective practice
Monitor candidate progress toward competency and professional growth goals
2. Coaching & Professional Support
Mentor candidates in instructional planning, classroom management, assessment, and differentiation
Support candidates in gradually assuming full teaching responsibilities
Encourage reflective practice and self-assessment
Provide resources, modeling, and guidance as needed to strengthen instructional practice
3. Collaboration & Communication
Maintain ongoing communication with the Mentor Teacher and school site staff
Collaborate with university faculty, program coordinators, and fieldwork administrators
Address concerns promptly and communicate any performance issues to program leadership
4. Evaluation & Documentation
Complete formative and summative evaluations of candidate performance
Participate in midterm and final evaluation conferences
Document observation data, progress, and competencies in required systems
Contribute to final assessment of candidate readiness for credential recommendation
5. Program Participation
Attend required supervisor trainings, meetings, and calibration sessions
Support candidates in meeting program milestones (e.g., CalTPA, lesson planning, portfolio development)
Ensure adherence to university and state credentialing requirements
Guidelines for Field Supervisors
Professional Standards:Uphold confidentiality, ethical practice, and professional conduct at all times
Consistency & Reliability:Complete observations, feedback, and documentation within required timelines
Constructive Feedback:Balance strengths-based feedback with clear, growth-oriented recommendations
Collaboration:Foster a positive working relationship with candidates and mentor teachers; operate as part of a support team
Responsiveness:Address candidate needs and concerns proactively; escalate concerns when necessary
Equity & Inclusion:Support candidates in implementing inclusive, culturally responsive, and legally compliant instructional practices for diverse learners
Reflective Practice:Model ongoing professional reflection and continuous improvement
Big Picture
There’s a quiet tension in this role—you’re both coach and evaluator. You’re helping someone grow while also deciding if they’re ready.
Handled well, it doesn’t feel like gatekeeping. It feels more like… standing just close enough that they don’t fall, but far enough that they learn how to stand on their own.
